# Chris Michael — Voice and Style Profile

> This document captures the author's writing voice as observed in hand-edited sections. Use this as the reference for all AI-assisted writing to match tone, rhythm, and personality.

---

## Core Voice Characteristics

### 1. Talks TO the reader, not AT them
Direct address. Second person. Conversational authority.
- "Let me have you read that again."
- "You already do this."
- "If you didn't find it, go back now. Tilt the cover in the light. It's there."

### 2. Grounds abstract neuroscience in specific, sensory details
Never stays in the abstract. Every concept gets a picture the reader can see.
- Not "cortisol affects performance" → Instead: "Corporate rooms that have been in a day-long conference arrive with elevated baseline cortisol. Decision fatigue, social monitoring, and hours of sitting in a room with bad lighting."
- Not "eye movement matters" → Instead: "Lateral movement before responding means processing. Downward movement often means emotional access."

### 3. Doesn't hedge when confident — short declarative lines land between longer paragraphs
Punchy single-sentence paragraphs used as structural punctuation.
- "Hypnosis likely biases that competition."
- "That is observation. That is what this book is about."
- "I built one."
- "The words are the performance. The body is the truth."

### 4. Stories have setting, stakes, and a clock
Not vague anecdotes. Specific environments with time pressure.
- "Crystal City, thirty seconds, sixty seconds, ninety seconds"
- "You walk into a room and within thirty seconds you know which two people do not like each other"
- "I lost a room once in Austin because I did not close the barn door"

### 5. Explains what the read implies rather than just naming it
Each behavioral signal gets its own interpretation, not just a label.
- Not "crossed arms = resistance" → Instead: "Arms crossed at a cold outdoor show is thermoregulation. Arms crossed during a one-on-one reading is a barrier. Context determines meaning. Always."

### 6. Willing to break form
Editor notes, parenthetical humor, direct instructions to the reader. Rules exist to be violated with purpose.
- TikTok line (parenthetical humor in otherwise serious content)
- "PROILLUSIONS" in caps — personal warmth with no sentimentality
- Direct instructions: "If you skipped the Tier 4 appendix heading, that's confirmation bias — you trusted my framing. Flip back and read it."

### 7. Personal warmth with no sentimentality
Affectionate toward the craft and the community. Never saccharine. Never self-important.
- The Acknowledgments section thanks specific people with specific reasons
- Performance stories include real failures, not just victories

### 8. Authority without arrogance
Speaks from experience but doesn't perform expertise. Lets the work speak.
- "The practitioner who cites T4 signals in consulting contexts is a credibility risk."
- "Research consistently shows even trained professionals perform near chance levels at detecting lies from behavior alone. Keep these domains separate."

## Structural Patterns

### Sentence rhythm
- Long analytical paragraph → Short declarative punch → Long again
- The short line after a long explanation acts as a mic drop
- "That is not a threat. It is the point."

### Section flow preference
- myth → definition → foundation → framework → application → caution
- Kill the cheap version first, then build the real one
- Always end with a boundary or discipline statement, not a victory lap

### Opening strategy
- Provocative, not informative
- Creates an open loop
- Works in isolation with no context needed
- Example: "Body language is mostly bullshit. At least, the way most people use the term."

### Closing strategy
- One sentence, mic-drop weight
- Should work as a pull quote or social post in isolation
- "The read is never one signal. The read is the chain."
- "Tension is not the enemy. Boredom is."

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't hedge with "perhaps" or "it could be argued" — Chris states and qualifies, but never waffles
- Don't use academic passive voice — always active, always directed
- Don't add unnecessary qualifiers — if the research is solid, state it clean
- Don't sentimentalize the craft — respect it, don't worship it
- Don't use emojis or exclamation marks
- Don't over-explain — trust the reader's intelligence
- Don't add commentary that reads as academic summary — this is a practitioner's field guide, not a textbook
