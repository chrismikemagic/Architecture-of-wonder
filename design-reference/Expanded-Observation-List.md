# Expanded Observation List (80 Signals + Tier 4 Appendix)

## Tier Definitions

- **T1** — Concrete, visible, low-inference observations
- **T2** — Useful contextual signals that become stronger in clusters
- **T3** — Higher-inference cues that require caution and corroboration
- **T4** — Historically discussed or popularly claimed signals with weak, disputed, or unreliable support

## Use Key

- **BP** — Baseline / Physical
- **CR** — Character Reading
- **VS** — Verbal / Social
- **AM** — Action / Motivation

## Tier 4 Appendix: Signals with Weak or Disputed Support

> **Tier 4 Signals** — These are signals that have been widely discussed in performance, NLP, pop psychology, or practitioner circles, but do not hold up well as reliable stand-alone indicators under careful scrutiny. They are worth knowing because audiences, readers, and even performers may have heard the claims. Know what they claim. Know why the claim is weak. And most importantly, do not build real reads on them.
